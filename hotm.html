About Adesola

Adesola is a third-year Computer Science student at the University of Lethbridge with a passion for building technology that actually matters. Originally from Lagos, Nigeria, she moved to Canada to pursue her degree and found herself navigating a new country, a new academic system, and a new industry — all at once.

Before joining The Hive, Adesola describes feeling like she was always one step behind. "I knew the theory from class, but I had no idea how teams actually worked, how projects got done, or how to talk about what I could do." She spent her first year learning in isolation — watching tutorials, building small projects alone, unsure of where she fit.

That changed when she attended her first Hive event in the fall of 2024. Within a semester, she had contributed to two real projects, made connections across disciplines, and — for the first time — felt like she belonged in tech.

How She Showed Up For The Hive

Adesola joined The Hive as a general member but quickly became one of its most consistent contributors. She took on a development role in the Black History Month Community Project, building the front-end of a digital storytelling platform that reached over 300 students across campus.

Beyond her technical contributions, Adesola became a quiet anchor for her team. She showed up to every working session, helped newer members troubleshoot their code, and volunteered to lead stand-ups when the team needed direction. "She never made a big deal of it," says one teammate. "She just got things done and made everyone feel like they could too."

She also played a key role at LeadFest 2026, where she represented The Hive during the student showcase and presented her project work to industry professionals. Several attendees reached out to her afterward for informational interviews.
